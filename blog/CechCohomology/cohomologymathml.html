Presheaves and sheaves

First, let us recall what was the definition of a sheaf. We start with a topological space XX and consider the category Op(X)\mathrm{Op}(X) whose objects are open subsets U‚äÇXU\subset X and whose morphisms are given by set inclusions $U\hookrightarrow V$. This is a particular example of a site, a category endowed with a Grothendieck topology. In general, sheaves are defined over sites, but for the sake of simplicity, we will stick to the category Op(X)\mathrm{Op}(X).

Exact sequences of sheaves

Sheaf cohomology is defined (at least until degree 11) in general for sheaves of groups. Therefore, all the sheaves that we are going to consider from now on are indeed sheaves of groups.
